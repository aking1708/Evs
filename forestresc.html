Ecosystem is a biological community of interacting organisms and their physical environment. Healthy ecosystems provide clean water, pure air, regulate climate, recycle nutrients; hence we cannot live without an ecosystem and it must be conserved for the present and the future generations.

Ecosystem consists of biotic components like plants, animals etc., and abiotic components like inorganic matter etc. These different components interact with each other to form a fully functional ecosystem.

To conserve an ecosystem, the ESA (Endangered Species Act) was formulated in 1973 to protect and conserve the components of an ecosystem. Several indigenous plants and animals have been protected under this Act. These form an important part of forest resources. In the 1900-1940 period, there was huge exploitation of forest resources which led to the conception of Indian Forest Act, 1927 to consolidate and reserve areas having forest cover. Later, in 1980, Forest Act was conceptualized. Several medicinal plants are now extinct which could have saved us from several dangerous diseases. Forests and its resources provide us food, medicine, fuel, shelter etc. Therefore, it is our responsibility to use these resources with an ecological mindset as opposed to an economic one.
